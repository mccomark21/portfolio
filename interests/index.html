Interests

When I step away from the data, here's what you'll find me doing—or at least attempting to do.

Sports & Fantasy Leagues

I've always loved sports, and honestly the fantasy leagues are where my analytical side really comes out to play. I run multiple leagues with friends and family across football, baseball, basketball, and soccer—and yes, I take them seriously. It's basically applied data science with higher emotional stakes.

The one sport I actually play these days is golf. I am, by most objective measures, bad at it. But I'm out there every chance I get, grinding away, celebrating the occasional decent shot, and deeply committed to the delusion that I'm improving. I follow golf, soccer, basketball, baseball, and football pretty closely, so there's always something to watch.

Sci-Fi & Fantasy

I'm a lifelong fan of the big three: Star Wars, Lord of the Rings, and Harry Potter. There's something about epic world-building and the battle between good and evil that never gets old. I'm always on the lookout for the next great series in the genre—movies, shows, books—and I have strong opinions about all of them that I'm happy to share if you've got time.

Food, Coffee & Other Things That Matter

I come from a family that takes food seriously. My mother makes the best chicken cutlets I've ever had. My wife makes an incredible spicy pasta sauce. (These are facts. I stand by both.) My personal contribution to the table is garlic bread—elite level, I'm told repeatedly, and I believe it.

A couple years ago I went through what I can only describe as a quarter-ish life crisis and came out the other side with an espresso machine. I source my beans locally, my lattes are genuinely good, and I am still very much a work in progress on the latte art. The journey continues.

When I need to fully decompress, you'll find me at the beach. Salt air and no deadlines is basically the perfect combination.
